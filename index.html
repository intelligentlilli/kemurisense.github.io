The Issue

As people grow older, their families want to know that they are healthy and active. The fear is that accident and illness may be undetected for days, or even weeks.  People want to know wellbeing every day – are they keeping warm, eating, drinking and moving around as normal?

Families are now widely dispersed and everybody needs reassurance of wellbeing, without frequent or unwelcome intrusive phone calls.

Our solution

With our app, the families, friends or carers can view wellbeing  whenever they want,  wherever they are, by installing our KemuriSense Smart Power Socket.

They can see hourly and daily indicators of wellbeing of the elderly person for the past week and can react when things don’t seem to be going to plan.

Smart Monitoring

Kemuri provides reassurance with passive sensors and predictive monitoring. Our service learns daily patterns of activity and predicts what should happen in the next hour.

For example, if Grandpa has his morning tea at 7am every day but then on one morning doesn’t make it, we can notify everyone who is concerned for his welfare, which might result in a phonecall to check up on him. It may not have been a problem, but it’s good to be sure.

KemuriSense

Kemuri informs family friends and carers whether a vulnerable person is warm, eating, drinking and moving around as normal – to get peace of mind.

 

People use their kettles and microwaves as normal. KemuriSense® smart power sockets measure temperature, power usage, motion, and power supply. Data is sent continuously to the Internet without using broadband and even during power cuts. Non-stop operation. It’s common sense.

Installation

KemuriSense® smart power sockets are professionally installed in the kitchen. There’s no change in lifestyle, nothing to remember and no intrusive monitoring. It works like a standard double power socket. Smart software checks activity every hour, identifies changes and keeps everybody informed via the Internet. Nothing could be simpler.

The product is most suitable for people who do not wish to be reminded of their increasing frailty by wearing an alarm pendant. They have to appoint a trusted person, or organisation, who can share the data with family members, friends or carers. No data will be shared without full consent.

Independence

The Kemuri service assists during the phase of life where living alone becomes gradually more difficult but still very much possible. Staying longer in one’s own home means a continuation of an independent and fulfilling lifestyle.
